By JENNA WORTHAM Ever wish you could get a text message every time someone tagged you in a Facebook photo?Or that you could record notes to yourself that are transcribed and sent to your e-mail account?A nifty new Web service called ifttt (pronounced “lift” but minus the l) offers a way to automate tasks involving a bevy of services like Instagram, Craiglist, Dropbox and Instapaper, among others.The basic premise of the service is simple.Users simply connect two services together to respond to triggers, under the construct that if this happens, then do that.
